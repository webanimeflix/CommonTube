Analysis of Financial Statements

1. Introduction to Financial Statement Analysis

Financial statement analysis helps in understanding the financial health of a company. (Financial statement analysis ek process hai jo company ki financial sthiti ko samajhne me madad karta hai.)

2. Objectives of Financial Statement Analysis

  • Assessing profitability. (Company ke profit ka analysis karna.)
  • Evaluating financial stability. (Company ki financial sthirata ko dekhna.)
  • Understanding liquidity position. (Company ke short-term payments ki sthiti dekhna.)
  • Helping in decision-making. (Management ke decisions me madad karna.)

3. Tools of Financial Statement Analysis

  • Comparative Statements: Compare financial data over different years. (Financial data ka alag-alag saalon ka tulnatmak vishleshan.)
  • Common Size Statements: Express financial items as a percentage of a base amount. (Har item ko ek base amount ke percentage me dikhana.)
  • Trend Analysis: Identifies trends over time. (Samay ke sath hone wale financial changes ka analysis.)
  • Ratio Analysis: Uses financial ratios to evaluate performance. (Financial ratios ka upayog karke performance evaluate karna.)
  • Cash Flow Statement: Shows inflow and outflow of cash. (Cash ke aane aur jaane ka record dikhata hai.)

4. Types of Ratios

  • Liquidity Ratios: Measure short-term financial position. (Short-term financial position ka analysis.)
  • Solvency Ratios: Measure long-term stability. (Long-term stability ko check karne wale ratios.)
  • Profitability Ratios: Measure the ability to generate profit. (Profit kamane ki kshamata ka analysis.)
  • Turnover Ratios: Measure efficiency in using resources. (Resources ka sahi upayog kitna ho raha hai, iska analysis.)

5. Importance of Financial Statement Analysis

Financial analysis helps investors, creditors, and management in making informed decisions. (Financial analysis investors, creditors, aur management ko sahi faisle lene me madad karta hai.)
