Money and Banking - Detailed Summary

Money: Money is anything that is generally accepted as a medium of exchange, store of value, unit of account, and standard of deferred payment. (Paise ka kaam hota hai cheezein kharidna, value store karna, hisaab rakhna aur udhaar ka bhi kaam aana.)

Functions of Money:

  • Medium of Exchange: Money allows people to buy and sell goods/services. (Paisa hone se barter system ki dikkat nahi hoti.)
  • Store of Value: Money retains value over time. (Paisa rakhne se future me bhi use kar sakte hain.)
  • Unit of Account: Money helps measure the value of goods/services. (Har cheez ki ek fixed keemat hoti hai jo paise me hoti hai.)
  • Standard of Deferred Payment: Money can be used to settle debts in the future. (Loan ya udhaar money ki wajah se possible hota hai.)

Banking System:

Banks play a key role in the economy by accepting deposits, providing loans, and facilitating transactions.

Types of Banks:

  • Commercial Banks: These banks accept deposits from the public and provide loans. (Yeh banks logon ka paisa rakhte hain aur loan bhi dete hain.)
  • Central Bank: The Reserve Bank of India (RBI) controls money supply and banking regulations. (RBI saare banks ko control karta hai aur naye note print karta hai.)

Credit Creation:

Banks create credit by lending more than the deposits they hold, based on reserve ratios set by the central bank. (Banks zyada loan dete hain jitna paisa deposit me hota hai, isse naye paise bante hain.)

Monetary Policy:

  • CRR (Cash Reserve Ratio): The percentage of total deposits that banks must keep as reserves with the RBI. (Banks ko apne kuch paise RBI me rakhna padta hai.)
  • SLR (Statutory Liquidity Ratio): The percentage of deposits banks must keep in liquid assets like gold and bonds. (Bank ke kuch paise safe assets me rakhne padte hain.)
  • Repo Rate: The interest rate at which banks borrow money from the RBI. (Jab banks ko paise ki zaroorat hoti hai to RBI se loan lete hain.)
  • Reverse Repo Rate: The interest rate at which banks deposit their excess funds with the RBI. (Jab banks ke paas extra paisa hota hai to RBI ke paas rakh dete hain.)

Importance of Banking System:

  • Helps in economic growth by providing loans.
  • Facilitates smooth functioning of trade and business.
  • Regulates money supply in the economy.
  • Encourages saving and investment.
