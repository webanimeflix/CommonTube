Accounting for Companies

1. Introduction to Companies

A company is a legal entity formed by a group of individuals to engage in business activities. (Company ek legal entity hoti hai jo ek ya adhik logon dwara business karne ke liye banayi jati hai.)

2. Types of Companies

  • Private Company: A company that restricts share transfer and has limited members. (Ek aisi company jo share transfer ko restrict karti hai aur jisme members ki sankhya seemit hoti hai.)
  • Public Company: A company that allows public share trading and has no member limit. (Ek aisi company jo public ko shares kharidne aur bechne ki anumati deti hai aur jisme members ki koi seema nahi hoti.)
  • Government Company: A company in which at least 51% of shares are owned by the government. (Ek aisi company jisme kam se kam 51% shares sarkar ke paas hote hain.)

3. Share Capital

The total capital of a company is divided into shares. (Company ki total capital ko shares me divide kiya jata hai.)

  • Authorized Capital: Maximum capital a company is allowed to raise. (Wo maximum capital jo company raise kar sakti hai.)
  • Issued Capital: Capital offered to the public for subscription. (Wo capital jo public ke liye offer kiya jata hai.)
  • Subscribed Capital: Capital actually taken by the public. (Wo capital jo public ne subscribe kiya hai.)
  • Paid-up Capital: The amount actually received from shareholders. (Wo capital jo shareholders se mil chuka hai.)

4. Issue of Shares

Companies issue shares to raise funds. (Company funds raise karne ke liye shares issue karti hai.)

  • At Par: Issued at face value. (Face value par issue kiya jata hai.)
  • At Premium: Issued at a price higher than face value. (Face value se zyada price par issue hota hai.)
  • At Discount: Issued at a price lower than face value. (Face value se kam price par issue hota hai.)

5. Issue of Debentures

Debentures are long-term loans taken by companies. (Debentures company dwara liye gaye long-term loans hote hain.)

  • Secured Debentures: Backed by company assets. (Company ki assets ke dwara secured hote hain.)
  • Unsecured Debentures: Not backed by any assets. (Kisi bhi asset se secured nahi hote.)
  • Convertible Debentures: Can be converted into shares. (Shares me convert kiye ja sakte hain.)

6. Final Accounts of a Company

Companies prepare financial statements to show their performance. (Company apni financial position dikhane ke liye financial statements banati hai.)

  • Profit and Loss Account: Shows company’s profits or losses. (Company ke profit ya loss ko dikhata hai.)
  • Balance Sheet: Shows company’s assets and liabilities. (Company ki assets aur liabilities ko dikhata hai.)
