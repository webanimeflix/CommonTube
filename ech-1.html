National Income and Related Aggregates - Detailed Summary

National Income: National income refers to the total monetary value of all goods and services produced within a country in a given period, usually one year. (Ek desh ek saal me jitna maal aur services banata hai, uska total value.)

Important Concepts:

  • Gross Domestic Product (GDP): Total value of all final goods and services produced within the country in a specific period. (Sirf final maal aur services jo ek desh ke andar banta hai, uska total.)
  • Gross National Product (GNP): GDP + Net income from abroad. (GDP me wo paisa add hota hai jo desh ke log bahar kama ke laate hain, aur jo foreign log yahan kama rahe hain usko minus karte hain.)
  • Net National Product (NNP): GNP - Depreciation. (Jo purane machines ya assets ki value kam hoti hai, usko ghata dete hain.)
  • National Disposable Income: NNP + Net transfers from the rest of the world. (Jo paisa humko bina kisi kaam ke milta hai, jaise remittances ya donations, usko add karte hain.)
  • Personal Income: Total income received by individuals before paying taxes. (Logon ka total kamaai tax dene se pehle.)
  • Disposable Income: Personal Income - Direct Taxes. (Jo paisa logon ke haath me bachta hai tax katne ke baad.)

Methods of Measuring National Income:

  • Production Method: It calculates the value of all goods and services produced in the economy. (Isme jitne bhi cheezein banti hain, unka total value calculate kiya jata hai.)
  • Income Method: It calculates the total earnings of factors of production (land, labor, capital, and entrepreneurship). (Jo log paisa kama rahe hain, jaise mazdoor, businessman, investor, unka income calculate hota hai.)
  • Expenditure Method: It measures the total expenditure on final goods and services in the economy. (Log jitna paisa kharche kar rahe hain, wo add karte hain.)

Importance of National Income:

  • It helps to measure the economic growth of a country.
  • It helps the government to make policies.
  • It helps in comparing the economy of different countries.
  • It helps in understanding the standard of living of people.
