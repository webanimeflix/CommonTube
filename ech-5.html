Balance of Payments - Detailed Summary

Balance of Payments (BoP): A record of all economic transactions between residents of a country and the rest of the world in a given period. (Yeh ek financial report hoti hai jo batati hai ki desh ka kitna paisa andar aa raha hai aur kitna bahar ja raha hai.)

Components of BoP:

  • Current Account: Records export and import of goods and services, income, and transfers.
  • Capital Account: Records transactions related to investment and loans.
  • Financial Account: Tracks changes in foreign exchange reserves and foreign investments.

Current Account Details:

  • Trade Balance: Difference between export and import of goods.
  • Invisible Balance: Net earnings from services, transfers, and income.

Capital Account Details:

  • Foreign Direct Investment (FDI) inflows and outflows.
  • Loans and borrowings from foreign institutions.
  • Foreign aid and grants.

Balance of Payments Deficit:

When total outflows (payments) exceed total inflows (receipts), it leads to a BoP deficit. (Jab desh se paisa bahar zyada jata hai aur andar kam aata hai, toh BoP deficit hota hai.)

Measures to Correct BoP Disequilibrium:

  • Promoting exports through incentives.
  • Reducing unnecessary imports.
  • Attracting foreign investment.
  • Maintaining foreign exchange reserves.
