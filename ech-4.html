Government Budget and the Economy - Detailed Summary

Government Budget: A government budget is an annual financial statement showing expected revenue and expenditure of the government. (Sarkar ka ek plan hota hai jisme bataaya jata hai ki kitna paisa aayega aur kahan kharch hoga.)

Objectives of Government Budget:

  • Economic Growth: Encouraging development through investments.
  • Reducing Inequality: Redistribution of wealth via taxes and subsidies.
  • Economic Stability: Controlling inflation and unemployment.
  • Efficient Resource Allocation: Prioritizing important sectors like health and education.

Components of Budget:

  • Revenue Budget: Includes governmentâ€™s income and day-to-day expenses.
  • Capital Budget: Includes long-term investments and borrowings.

Types of Government Expenditure:

  • Revenue Expenditure: Recurring expenses like salaries, pensions, subsidies.
  • Capital Expenditure: Investment in infrastructure, machinery, public projects.

Types of Government Revenue:

  • Tax Revenue: Money collected from taxes like income tax, GST, excise duty.
  • Non-Tax Revenue: Income from government services, fines, and interest earnings.

Fiscal Deficit:

Fiscal deficit occurs when government expenditure exceeds revenue. It is managed through borrowings. (Jab sarkar ka kharcha uski aamdani se zyada hota hai, toh usse fiscal deficit kehte hain.)

Types of Budget:

  • Balanced Budget: When income and expenditure are equal.
  • Surplus Budget: When income is more than expenditure.
  • Deficit Budget: When expenditure is more than income.

Government Measures to Manage Deficit:

  • Increasing taxes to generate more revenue.
  • Reducing unnecessary government expenditure.
  • Borrowing from domestic and international sources.
  • Encouraging private sector participation in development.
