Development Experience (1947-90) - Detailed Summary

Economic Development After Independence: After independence, India adopted a planned economic system to promote growth and reduce poverty. (Azadi ke baad, Bharat ne ek planned economy ko apnaya taaki vikas ho aur gareebi kam ho.)

Objectives of Economic Planning:

  • Economic Growth: Increase in GDP and national income.
  • Poverty Alleviation: Reducing poverty through employment programs.
  • Self-Sufficiency: Reducing dependence on foreign imports.
  • Modernization: Improving technology in agriculture and industries.
  • Equity: Reducing income inequality.

Five-Year Plans (1951-1990):

  • First Five-Year Plan (1951-56): Focus on agriculture and irrigation.
  • Second Five-Year Plan (1956-61): Emphasis on industrialization.
  • Third Five-Year Plan (1961-66): Focus on self-reliance and economic stability.
  • Fourth & Fifth Plans: Addressed poverty and employment.

Agricultural Development:

The Green Revolution (1960s) introduced high-yield variety (HYV) seeds, fertilizers, and irrigation, improving food production. (Green Revolution ne naye techniques laake kheti me badlav kiya.)

Industrial Development:

  • Public sector enterprises were established.
  • Import substitution policy reduced foreign dependency.

Trade and Foreign Policy:

India followed a protectionist trade policy, imposing high tariffs to encourage domestic industries. (Desh ne import duties lagakar apne industries ko protect kiya.)

Limitations of the Planning Period:

  • Slow economic growth.
  • High poverty and unemployment.
  • Inefficient public sector enterprises.
