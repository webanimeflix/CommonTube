Cash Flow Statement

1. Introduction to Cash Flow Statement

A cash flow statement shows the movement of cash in and out of a business. (Cash flow statement ek aisa record hai jo business me aane aur jaane wale cash ka pata lagata hai.)

2. Objectives of Cash Flow Statement

  • Shows liquidity position. (Company ke cash availability ka pata lagata hai.)
  • Helps in financial planning. (Future financial planning me madad karta hai.)
  • Evaluates cash-generating capacity. (Company ki cash kamaane ki kshamata ka analysis karta hai.)
  • Helps in decision-making. (Investments aur loans ke decisions lene me madad karta hai.)

3. Activities in Cash Flow Statement

  • Operating Activities: Cash flows from core business operations. (Company ke main business se hone wale cash inflow aur outflow.)
  • Investing Activities: Cash flows from buying or selling assets. (Assets kharidne ya bechne se hone wale cash transactions.)
  • Financing Activities: Cash flows related to funding and capital. (Loans, debentures, aur shares issue karne ya repay karne se sambandhit cash flow.)

4. Importance of Cash Flow Statement

It helps stakeholders understand the cash position of a company. (Ye stakeholders ko company ki cash position samajhne me madad karta hai.)
